SqueezeNet

Jun 26, 2016 | Hits

Implementing SqueezeNet in Torch.

The main idea behind SqueezeNet is:

  • Replace some $3\times 3$ CONV kernels with $1\times 1$s.
  • Decrease the # of CONV kernels with a bottleneck block called ‘Fire Module’.

Fire Module

Say we have a CONV layer with nInputPlane = 96 and nOutputPlane = 128. And if we use $3\times 3$ kernels, we need 96*128*3*3=110592 parameters.

The ‘Fire Module’ works as:

  1. First ‘squeeze’ the nInputPlane to a small nSqueezePlane say $16$.
  2. Then ‘expand’ the nSqueezePlane back to nOutputPlane by concating two parts:
    • Feature map with $1\times 1$ kernels, CONV channels = nOutputPlane/2
    • Feature map with $3\times 3$ kernels, CONV channels = nOutputPlane/2

With ‘Fire Module’ we just need: 96*64*1*1+96*64*3*3=61440 that is $45\%$ less.

  • Fire Module可以看成是bottlenect network的一种,而且这种concat补齐维度的做法在ResNet中也有用到.
  • SqueezeNet和ResNet都抛弃了FC层,改用AveragePooling代替,trend.
